WHY THIS
MATTERS

When cocaine and alcohol are taken together, they form a new substance called cocaethylene. This super drug is stronger than either cocaine or alcohol. Cocaethylene can stay in your body for days, even weeks, depending on how much you take.


It might feel like you're on a high, full of energy and confidence. But the risks are serious. Besides posing several physical risks, such as increased risks to heart and liver health, cocaethlyene causes:

  • Worsening trauma outcome
  • Increased impulsivity
  • Suicidal ideation and attempt
  • Anxiety
  • Panic attacks
  • Increased Depression

According to study in 2022a, 21% of all drug-related emergency visits in the year were from mixing substances. The most common combo? Cocaine and alcohol. Mixing them increases the risk of sudden death to up to 25%. The powerful psychoactive effects of both cocaine and cocaethylene, produces feelings of energy, focus, and excitement increasing impulsivity and risk-taking.
